Who are we

We are the AENN, a nonprofit organization which has the aim of spreading knowledge about neuroscience field in Chile, considering that a formal neuroscience degree does not exist in our country. However, we hope thay someday neuroscience will be more relevant in education.

Origin of AENN

AENN's origin as a group of friends who were studying at university and shared a common interest for neuroscience. They wanted to show the lack of neursocience education in Chilean universities, as well as spreading scientific knowledge within student communities.

Principles

Student Association of Neuroscience and Neurotechnology has five values that need to be respected in all activities:

To democratize knowledge

Commitment to sharing information in a transparent and accessible manner, promoting openness and understanding.

Integrity

Maintain high ethical standards and act with honesty and consistency in all interactions and decisions.

Equality

Ensuring fair and equitable treatment for all, regardless of differences, promoting inclusion and diversity.

Respect

Recognizing and valuing individuality and the opinions of others, fostering an environment of tolerance and mutual consideration.

Benevolence

Actively seeking well-being and positive contribution, working for the benefit of all parties involved.

Our mission

Building a community that creates interdisciplinary spaces around neuroscience and neurotechnology to facilitate access to these disciplines.

Our vision

To be a national community of neuroscience and neurotechnology that connects all individuals in the country interested in these disciplines.

Our goal

Bringing the knowledge of neuroscience and neurotechnology closer to students from various disciplines through the organization of academic activities and scientific outreach, with the aim of democratizing neuroscience knowledge and culture.

Coordination and Leadership

Ignacio Jaramillo, President

Ignacio Jaramillo, current president of the Student Association of Neuroscience and Neurotechnology, is immersed in his bachelor's degree in Psychology at the Autonomous University of Chile. His approach encompasses a cross-sectional perspective of neuroscience. He holds diplomas and a postgraduate degree in microbiota, with a special emphasis on clinical psychoneuroimmunology. His main purpose is to establish diplomatic connections between the various disciplines that make up neuroscience and neurotechnology, directing his research towards the field of microbiota. He aspires to continue expanding his academic horizon with dedication and rigor.

Pablo Muñoz, Vice President

Pablo Muñoz is a Mathematical Civil Engineer from the Federico Santa María Technical University and a Master's student in Mathematics at the same institution. Additionally, he is a student in the Diploma program in the philosophy of science with a focus on the philosophy of complex systems. His interests lie in the philosophy of mind and computational neuroscience. In the future, he would like to work on bridging these areas through mathematical and philosophical tools.

Tanhia Alvear, Treasurer

Tanhia Alvear is a biotechnology engineer from UTEM (University of Technology Metropolitana). She is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Neuroscience at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile. Her doctoral thesis is focused on studying the effect of α-synuclein on PANX1 channels in neuronal cells.

María Fernanda Cuevas, Coordinator of Well-being and Events Commission"

Fernanda Cuevas currently serves as the coordinator of the Well-being and Events committees in the Student Association of Neuroscience and Neurotechnology. She is a nursing student at the Universidad San Sebastián. Her interests include cognitive and behavioral neuroscience, as well as neurodegenerative diseases. Her goal is to popularize the knowledge of neuroscience so that it is accessible to everyone, and she would also like to contribute to research in the field of nursing.

Vicente Pinto, Coordinator of Communications and Social Media Commission

Vicente Pinto is a psychologist from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile. His interests include the study of the therapeutic use of psychedelic substances, as well as understanding how neurobiological effects could explain the functioning of the mind.

Diego Bastías, Coordinator of Process Management Unit
